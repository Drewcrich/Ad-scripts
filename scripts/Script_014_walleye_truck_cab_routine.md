## Caption Notes (CapCut)

**IMPORTANT:** Add auto-captions to all three clips for accessibility and engagement. Captions increase watch time and make content accessible.

### Caption Settings in CapCut

1. **Auto-Generate Captions:**
   - Import all clips into CapCut
   - Select all clips
   - Go to "Text" ‚Üí "Auto Captions"
   - Language: English
   - Let CapCut generate captions automatically

2. **Caption Style:**
   - **Font:** Bold, sans-serif (e.g., "Montserrat Bold" or "Bebas Neue")
   - **Size:** Large enough to read on mobile (adjust for 9:16 vertical format)
   - **Color:** White text with black outline/shadow for readability
   - **Position:** Lower third (bottom 25% of screen) to avoid covering face and screenshot overlay
   - **Animation:** Word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase pop-in (keeps attention)
   - **Background:** Optional semi-transparent black bar behind text for extra readability

3. **Caption Timing:**
   - **Clip 1 (0-12s):** Captions for entire dialogue
   - **Clip 2 (12-27s):** Captions for entire dialogue
   - **Clip 3 (27-42s):** Captions for entire dialogue
   - Ensure captions sync perfectly with speech (adjust timing if needed)

4. **Key Phrases to Emphasize:**
   - Clip 1: "I don't even back down the driveway" (make this line stand out)
   - Clip 2: "8:15 to 10:15 PM" and "window was spot on" (emphasize timing)
   - Clip 3: "100% real data," "bite windows and ratings," and "we rate how good each bite window is" (emphasize credibility and rating system)

5. **Caption Best Practices:**
   - Keep captions on screen for full duration of spoken words
   - Use all caps for emphasis on key phrases (optional)
   - Ensure captions don't cover the app screenshot overlay (position lower if needed)
   - Test readability on mobile device before posting
